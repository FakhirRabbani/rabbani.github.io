My journey with Graphics

My journey in graphics design has been a multifaceted exploration, commencing during my high school years and evolving through a combination of diverse classes and coursework. From the early stages of experimenting with visual elements to the nuanced understanding cultivated in dedicated graphics classes, my proficiency in graphic design has been a product of both formal education and hands-on experience. During high school, I first delved into the world of graphics, developing a fascination for Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator. These foundational experiences ignited my passion for visual storytelling and sparked my interest in the dynamic interplay of colors, shapes, and typography. The pursuit of mastering graphics design continued in university, where I enrolled in specialized courses. These classes not only provided structured learning environments but also allowed for deeper exploration of advanced techniques and industry-standard tools. Notably, one of these university courses, where I achieved an A grade, became a pivotal point in honing my skills. The university setting offered an immersive experience, providing access to cutting-edge design tools and collaboration with peers and mentors. Through challenging projects, critique sessions, and exposure to contemporary design trends, I refined my abilities to conceptualize and execute visually compelling designs. Complementing formal education, I further expanded my graphics knowledge by participating in additional classes and workshops outside the university setting. This multifaceted approach, blending formal education with self-directed learning, equipped me with a well-rounded skill set in graphics design, enabling me to approach creative projects with both technical expertise and artistic flair.
